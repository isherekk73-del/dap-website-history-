Department of Architecture and Planning

Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee

History of the Department

The Department of Architecture and Planning (DAP) at IIT Roorkee has a rich and distinguished history that dates back to 1956, making it one of the oldest architecture departments in India. Initially established as the Department of Architecture at the then University of Roorkee, DAP has been at the forefront of architectural education and research in the country.

The DAP legacy represents over six decades of excellence in architectural education, research, and practice in India.

When the University of Roorkee transformed into the Indian Institute of Technology Roorkee in 2001, the Department of Architecture evolved into the Department of Architecture and Planning (DAP), expanding its scope to include urban planning and regional development alongside traditional architectural education.

Key Milestones

1956 - Foundation

The Department of Architecture was established at the University of Roorkee, becoming one of the pioneering architecture schools in India. DAP's foundation marked the beginning of formal architectural education in North India.

1960s-1970s - Growth and Expansion

DAP expanded its curriculum and facilities during this period, introducing specialized courses in building construction, architectural design, and urban studies. The department established itself as a center of excellence for architectural education.

1980s-1990s - Modernization

The department modernized its teaching methodology and infrastructure. DAP introduced computer-aided design (CAD) and updated its laboratories to meet contemporary architectural practice standards.

2001 - IIT Roorkee Transformation

With the conversion of University of Roorkee to IIT Roorkee, the department became the Department of Architecture and Planning (DAP), gaining enhanced status and resources as part of the prestigious IIT system.

2000s-Present - Research and Innovation

DAP has become a hub for cutting-edge research in sustainable architecture, heritage conservation, urban planning, and smart cities. The department continues to produce graduates who shape India's built environment.

Academic Programs

Today, DAP offers comprehensive programs including Bachelor of Architecture (B.Arch), Master of Architecture (M.Arch) with various specializations, Master of Planning (M.Plan), and Ph.D. programs. The department is known for its rigorous curriculum that balances design excellence with technical proficiency and sustainable practices.

DAP continues to be recognized nationally and internationally for its contribution to architectural education, research, and professional practice.

The faculty at DAP comprises accomplished architects, planners, and researchers who bring extensive academic and professional experience. The department maintains strong links with professional bodies, industry, and international institutions, ensuring that its programs remain relevant and globally competitive.
